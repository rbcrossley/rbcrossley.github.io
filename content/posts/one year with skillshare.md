A year earlier, I subscribed to skillshare. If you're here, you already know what is skillshare. It's a website for online learning.

If you wonder how I purchased skillshare although I am from Nepal where paypal is not available, I have used dollar card from NMB bank in order to purchase skillshare from Nepal.

My reasons for purchasing skillshare were to have an affordable medium to learn. At that point in time, I was learning loads of concepts and buying each course in udemy was getting very expensive to me. Thus, I thought investing in skillshare would be worth the `$$`.

Skillshare is worth the `$$` for me. But I will mention why later on because what is worth for me might not be worth for you.

But, you should never purchase skillshare if you are looking to learn IT and Software development. For example things like Linux, software development, devops engineering, web development etc. The reason behind this is there are barely any courses to learn these skills. Even if there are courses for it, the courses are very short in duration and mostly garbage. You can't learn docker in 30 minutes video. Only difference is that it's actually worser than youtube.

Skillshare is worth it for me because of the two reasons.

- I didn't purchase skillshare for learning programming, software development, web development or devops related skills. i.e I didn't purchased skillshare for anything related to IT.
- I purchased skillshare for personal development. And I got good value with reference to that.

If I could go back on time, I'd still be purchasing skillshare. But I won't purchase it for anything more than 10$ per year. It's not worth above that sum for me as a Nepalese person.
