the idea for this post came around when a member of my discord server dmed me regarding how to prepare for loksewa in 3-6 months. btw, If you have not joined that discord server yet, please [join](https://discord.gg/hJt3gTQakB).

I had to be honest. 3 months of preparation is not enough for most average people. That might set a wrong impression that you can crack loksewa exams with 3 months of preparation. I am not saying people have not cracked by studying properly within that time however. I have personally seen lots of talented folks that have cracked Loksewa Computer Engineer examinations itself within that time. 

If you are fully directed, fully motivated,100% efficient then 6 months of full time study can be sufficient. 

The biggest problem that aspirants face is "which corporation syllabus should I prepare first?". this is a big confusion because loksewa vacancies in specific field are unpredictable. The exams occur within around 3 months of vacancy publication. And 3 months is not enough time for studying entire corporation curricula.

but do not worry.

It is true that the loksewa computer engineer syllabus although is very vast and differs corporation wise. But there is a pattern. There are some common subjects in every corporations.

I have mentioned this in this [detailed blog](https://berojgarengineer.com/blog/loksewa-computer-engineer-and-it-officer-in-nepal/).

loksewa computer engineer/IT officer exam contains two papers(except in NRB which is 3 papers).

The first paper could be about GK, IQ, corporation specific knowledge and management knowledge. Do not worry about the first paper currently.

Firstly, Focus on the second paper i.e. the technical part.

Do not get me wrong though. First paper is easier to score. And it is what differentiates people who crack vs people who do not crack loksewa exam.

But since you will be just starting out, focus on the technical part first. After six months, start focusing on the first paper. For example: you study the technical part for six months. Complete it properly. Now a vacancy opens. Now what you have to do is to study the first paper topics of that syllabus. It will not take more than one month to study the first paper if you do it full time.



The Common subjects in technical part include:

- Computer Architecture / Computer Organization
- Operating System
- Computer Networks
- Data Structures & Algorithms
- Database Management System (DBMS)
- Software Engineering
- Programming (C / C++ / OOP / Java)
- Digital Logic / Digital Systems
- Cryptography & Network Security
- Emerging Technologies (AI, Big Data, Cloud, IoT)

There are 10 common technical subjects. You want to be prepared within 6 months. So you have to finish 3 subjects every 2 months. Note that this is just common subjects. 

While in month 2 if a vacancy for some corporations appear, then you can obviously follow its syllabi. And try to complete it. I will assume no vacancies appear within 6 months.

# How to study?
First thing first.

Loksewa computer engineering and IT officer exams test on breadth of knowledge. In B.E or BSc.CSIT you might have faced lots of numerical questions. Do not expect such numerical questions in Loksewa Computer Engineer and IT officer exam.

The hardest part of following Loksewa provided syllabus for Computer engineer and it officer is that the micro level syllabus is not clear based on the provided syllabus in many corporations. That causes genuine frustrations. I wasted a lot of time going too deep because of this reason.

While joining institute helped me heavily here, you do not have to if you do not want to.

You basically need an outline of topics to study. When I say IO Organization, what comes to your mind? The book on COA will constitute many different topics in this chapter. 

previous year question papers help in this situation. Note that You can get the [old questions](https://berojgarengineer.com/blog/pyq-loksewa/) from this link.

You can extrapolate based on the available old questions. that is one way. For me what helped was following loksewa computer engineering institute's notes. They were not anything extraordinary. But they provided a much needed structure. You could call this as micro syllabus as well.

I will personally release such study guides soon in this blog. It will consists of micro syllabus of every subjects in Loksewa Computer Engineering and IT Officer. Keep following the blog for more information. It will definitely take time though. Otherwise you can just think about the usual important questions while learning and study those.
